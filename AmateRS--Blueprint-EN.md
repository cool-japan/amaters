# **Project AmateRS: The Sovereign Data Infrastructure**

**Vision Paper: Reclaiming Digital Dignity through "Computation in the Dark"**

## **1. Executive Summary: The Crisis and The Solution**

### **The Crisis: "The Glass House Society"**

Today's internet society is built on a trade-off: convenience in exchange for privacy. Data stored in the cloud is constantly exposed in plaintext to system administrators, platform operators, and malicious attackers.

This is like living in a house without locks, leaving all your valuables inside with the curtains wide open. **"Data Dignity"** has been lost in our current reality.

### **The Solution: "AmateRS"**

AmateRS is a next-generation database infrastructure that enables us to extract value (computation results) from data without exposing its contents to anyone.

Like the sun goddess Amaterasu hiding in the rock cave (Iwato), data remains hidden within a robust cryptographic shell (the rock door). Yet the light (computational power) emanating from it continues to illuminate the world.

This represents a turning point in human history—resolving the previously incompatible trade-off between "privacy protection" and "data utilization."

### **A. "Code represents Law, but Law guides Code"**

AmateRS functions not merely as a data store, but as a **"law enforcement agency."**

* Access rights to data are granted not through human "contracts," but solely through mathematical "proofs."
* Legal concepts such as the "right to be forgotten (GDPR)" and "secrecy of communications (Article 21 of the Constitution)" are implemented not as best-effort obligations, but as **"physically inviolable"** guarantees at the system level.

### **B. "Trustless Trust"**

Traditional social systems are built on "trusting people (asking administrators)."

AmateRS is a system where **"you don't need to trust anyone, so no one can betray you."**

Even if administrators have malicious intent, even if state power intervenes, it is mathematically impossible to view the actual encrypted data. This **dramatically reduces the "cost of trust"** in society.

## **3. Social Implementation Scenarios (Use Cases)**

How will the world change when AmateRS is implemented in society?

### **Case 1: Global Healthcare & Genomic Data**

* **Challenge:** Personal DNA data and medical histories are ultimate privacy, but people are afraid to share them for drug discovery research due to leakage concerns.
* **The AmateRS World:**
  * Patients provide their DNA in encrypted form.
  * Pharmaceutical companies and AI perform "analysis" on the encrypted data to find candidate drug compounds.
  * **Result:** Humanity's medical care evolves without leaking a single bit of personal privacy.

### **Case 2: Supply Chain & National Security**

* **Challenge:** Inter-company transaction data and manufacturing process details are "trade secrets." However, there's pressure to visualize the entire supply chain's CO2 emissions and human rights risks.
* **The AmateRS World:**
  * Each company submits their secrets (costs, procurement sources) in encrypted form.
  * The system calculates and publishes only the "overall supply chain health score."
  * **Result:** An ethical economic sphere with high transparency is built while maintaining competitiveness.

### **Case 3: Financial Inclusion (Credit Scoring)**

* **Challenge:** People without bank accounts and young people cannot get loans due to lack of credit scores. However, there's resistance to exposing all payment and behavioral history.
* **The AmateRS World:**
  * Personal payment and behavioral data is accumulated in AmateRS in encrypted form.
  * Financial institutions only submit queries like "Is it safe to lend to this person?" and AmateRS returns only "Yes/No" and "recommended interest rate."
  * **Result:** A society where legitimate evaluation and opportunities are available while protecting privacy.
